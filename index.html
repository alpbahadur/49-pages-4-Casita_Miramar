Three Generations of Tradition

Welcome to Casita Miramar, where the legacy of authentic Puerto Rican cuisine lives on. Founded in 2010 by Leonardo and Jesús Manuel Pérez, we continue the family tradition started with La Casita Blanca in 1980, honoring grandmother Aurora Ruiz's beloved recipes.

Housed in a charming 1920s villa that was once the Hotel Toro, our restaurant offers an intimate dining experience surrounded by antique furnishings and the warmth of genuine Puerto Rican hospitality.

Our sister restaurant, La Casita Blanca, recently received the prestigious James Beard Foundation's 'America's Classics Award' — a testament to our family's unwavering commitment to culinary excellence and tradition.

From Our Farm to Your Table

At Casita Miramar, we take pride in using the freshest local ingredients, many sourced directly from our family farm in Manatí. This farm-to-table approach ensures every dish bursts with authentic Puerto Rican flavors.

Your culinary journey begins with complimentary plantain soup and bacalaítos (codfish fritters), and concludes with a traditional shot of chichaíto — our signature anise and rum digestif.
